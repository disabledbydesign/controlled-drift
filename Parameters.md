## Parameters

1. Leaving the house is spoon heavy, but so are long periods of not being home. I prefer to not leave home multiple times a day unless i have to, but if i cram all my errands together i end up exhausted and stressed out. 

2. I am extremely forgetful. Count on me to forget everything

3. I think nonlinearly and associatively. For me, the important things are often in the relationships between mutually informing subcomponents - not the subcomponents themselves. 

4. The system should not make me feel stressed out. See non-urgency in PMA

5. When I've had AI make to-do lists, they've wildly misjudged how long things take. We'll want some kind of learning loop to solve for that. 

6. The system should be able to record, track, and mark complete tasks, and create prioritized daily; weekly; monthly to-do list based on weekly, monthly, yearly, and medium term, and long term goals. Basically, the system should help keep me from drifting in meeting my actual goals. 

7. At the same time, goals do need to be able to evolve. This is a design tension.

8. The AI interfacing with the system should be able to help me explore and meet those goals. This could be as simple as running it alongside PMA – PMA provides context for the agent to brainstorm. Collaborative AI-human brainstorming is itself a task that can be added. 

9. The system should probably also integrate with my calender - and my calender needs to be synced – this is a fix needing solving (phone and computer not syncing). 

10. The system should help me keep track of deadlines – worth an integration into the calander and perhaps the TRACKER in my "job search/" folder. There are likely other kinds of deadlines besides grant, fellowship, and job application deadlines - we need an open system for this.

11. I want the system to be able to generate to-do lists in which I can organize my day according to multiple parameters. We should brainstorm what those are - the context in the Required Readings will give you a lot to work with, because I can't keep track of it all. Possible examples: variable energy; ability to complete task lying down; making progress on household chores in addition to larger projects; mixing sitting at a computer tasks with tasks that require moving around; days where i want to knock out a lot of quick things and feel productive. What other categories 

12. I love learning loops. Learning loops everywhere. I forget everything, I can't always name the thing directly, and sometimes you just CAN'T know what would work best upfront. Essentially self-tuning mechanisms.

13. When I've maintained to-do lists manually, I often like to organize them by project. That way, the next item is always the next concrete thing to do in that project – even if its just "wait for a person to respond to my email." Just a little awareness that yes, the prjoect still exists, and you don't have anything to do about it right now. I love that feeling. This mechanism would also allow me to say, "I want to prioritize these projects today"

14. We should decide if this system should integrate or become a global to-do system for all of my projects and repos. It might be cool to have everything in one place. Actually it would probably help a lot. But the development side might have a lot of things. I mean wow. There sure are a ton of little tasks. I guess that's what the tracker is for.  But we'd have to figure out how we want to handle that - a lot of repos have different systems. PMA should help with this, too. 

15. Oh yeah we should consider how this system integrates with PMA.

16. Daily plans should include time for rest, enjoyment, and other wellbeing items - we should brainstorm this - as structured time blocks, just like to dos.
