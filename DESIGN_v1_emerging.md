If you're picking this up fresh, these recur below and you may not reconstruct them from context alone:

- **June** — the person this tool is for. Disabled, autistic, ADHD scholar; easily overwhelmed by long documents; thinks nonlinearly/associatively; forgets readily; works without institutional support. Full profile: `~/Documents/GitHub/profile/JUNE_BLOCH_AGENT_BRIEFING.md`. Design-relevant synthesis: `SYNTHESIS_design_time.md` in this folder (read it first).
- **The "output format is the activation function for bias" finding** — June's own research result (from her Autograder4Canvas project). A *binary* classifier (concern / no-concern) systematically mis-flagged minoritized students doing excellent work, because the binary format forces a deficit frame ("did this meet the standard?") and forecloses "what is this student reaching for?" Changing the *output format* — to open-ended generative description rather than a binary/score — fixed it. The relevance here: a conventional to-do list is built out of exactly these flattening formats (done/not-done, priority 1–5, % complete, streaks), so each one risks importing the deficit frame. This is the single most important design principle for this tool; "flattening" below always refers to this.
- **crip-time** — a disability-justice account of time: variable capacity is the *baseline*, not an exception; rest is not lost time; non-linear/circular progress (returning to earlier points) is valid, not failure. The opposite of productivity-clock time. This tool is built around it.
- **Temporal sovereignty / "offer, don't impose"** — the cardinal interaction rule from crip-time: the system *offers* a reading of capacity or pacing; the user always holds final authority over their own time and state. Never "you should rest" — instead "crip-time suggests pausing; what's your capacity?"
- **seventh-gen / spiral / linear (temporal lenses)** — different ways of reading time, from June's Reframe system. *seventh-gen* = long-horizon, accountability to the future, no urgency (good for goals). *spiral* = returning to the same territory with deeper understanding (good for recurring/evolving projects — "you're not starting over, you're returning with more knowledge"). *linear* = sequential deadlines/timelines (use deliberately, not as the default — the default would be the ableist productivity-clock).
- **PMA (Propositional Memory Architecture)** — a *separate* project: a shared long-term memory system for June + AI, holding situated knowledge with its provenance and the relationships between pieces. Not yet running. Relevant here only at one seam (§7). Lives at `~/Documents/GitHub/propositional-memory-architecture/`.
- **satisfy/violate test** — a way of stating a design requirement borrowed from the PMA design docs: alongside what a requirement *says*, you state what would *satisfy* it vs. what would *violate* it. A requirement you can't violate is decoration. Used for the guards in §6.

## 0. The core telos (don't lose this)

**Alignment lives outside June's head.** The anxiety drop she's after comes from *trusting that what she's doing is aligned with her goals without having to hold all the goals, strategies, and choices in her head at once.* She told us directly: if you ask her "what is this project reaching for," that's hard to answer AND the answer comes out stripped, because she can't hold all the pieces at once. So the system must HOLD the goal–strategy–alignment *for* her — she can't be the one who carries it.

This makes the system **distributed cognition**: June + the AI layer + the stored `reaching_for` field jointly hold what no single part holds alone. "It lives outside my head" is the architecture, not a metaphor (posthumanist-feminist / cyborg framing; PMA's "mutual access infrastructure, accommodate-and-empower").

## 1. Architecture decision

**A Python system with an AI interface layer.**
- **Python core (the structured part):** the list, task/status tracking, the database (projects, tasks, capacity axes, relations), deadlines, calendar.
- **AI interface layer (the flexible part):** generates daily/weekly lists from parameters + current capacity state + goal-alignment; breaks tasks into trajectories; checks drift against `reaching_for`; does the reasoning that a strict Python rules-system couldn't do flexibly.
- **Learning loop:** required *because June can't articulate all her accessibility needs upfront* — the system self-tunes beyond what she can specify (duration estimates first; later, which axes/modes actually serve her). Its own design session.

Why this split: the list/tracking has real stakes (must be reliable, checkable, persistent → Python); the construction and judgment want flexibility (→ AI). Not "reinvent the wheel" on the list itself (the conventional form earns its place *here* because of the stakes — see §6 on why "de-tooth the conventional form" is partly right).

## 4. AI-assisted task breakdown (designed this session)

June names a task/project; the AI proposes a **trajectory of steps** so she doesn't enumerate them upfront and can see the whole arc.

1. **Living, not frozen.** AI lays out the trajectory; June accepts/edits/reorders; **re-runnable mid-stream** ("3 steps in, X changed, re-lay from here"). New steps emerging in process is the expected mode, not a plan failure.
2. **Drift-vs-flexibility, via `reaching_for` as ground truth (June's idea):**
   - *Drift* = a change that pulls away from what the project is reaching for.
   - *Flexibility* = a new step that still serves it, even if unplanned.
   - The AI judges proposed changes against `reaching_for` and only **surfaces** possible drift in permission-granting register ("heads up, this looks like it's pulling away from what you said this is for — intentional?"), never blocks. Drift may *be* the goal evolving (spiral time) — allowed; the system asks, doesn't enforce.
   - **This is the mechanism by which alignment lives outside her head:** stored `reaching_for` + AI checking against it = she stays aligned without holding the whole plan.
3. **Collapsible granularity (capacity-linked).** "Just the next step" (low-capacity / bed-day) vs. "the whole arc" (when she wants the map). Offered, switched by current state, never imposed.
4. **Each step gets capacity tags** → a trajectory becomes schedulable across states ("the 2 paper-steps you could do lying down today").
5. **Breakdown is where the duration learning-loop attaches.** Estimates ride on steps; actual-vs-estimated feeds the loop (Param #5).
6. **"Waiting on" steps are first-class.** Surfacing "the next thing is to wait" *is* June's beloved feeling (Param #13): the project exists, nothing to do right now, no guilt.

**Design elements / parameters (settled this session):**
- **Errand-batching with a spoon cap (Param #1).** Group leaving-house tasks so she goes out once — but cap the batch so it doesn't tip into the exhausting over-cram.
- **Rest IS checkable — with inverted friction (June's correction, 2026-06-14).** Earlier draft made rest non-checkable to avoid "failing at resting." June corrected: make it checkable. She won't feel she failed at resting, but a checkable rest item makes *not-resting* visible so she **notices the pattern** — "just a little bit extra friction for not resting." So: rest/wellbeing/enjoyment are first-class checkable blocks; the affect is oriented toward *noticing under-rest*, not toward failure. (Maintenance/reproductive-labor framing: rest and care are real labor, not the absence of work.)
- **"Feel-productive" days are legitimate, not lesser.** Quick-wins-for-momentum is a real ND need that can conflict with goal-progress. The system honors a quick-wins day without guilt — maybe weaving in *one* goal-aligned quick win. Offer, don't impose.
- **Household woven with project work** (Param #11) — chores are real labor, mixed into the day by domain/embodiment, not a second-class list.
- **Calendar + deadlines = open, typed system** (Param #9/#10); the phone/computer calendar-sync fix is a real *prerequisite dependency*, scoped separately.

## 6. Non-negotiable guards (the political/accessibility spine)

These are the satisfy/violate boundaries (PMA-style) — violating one means the tool reproduced what it was built to refuse:

- **Nothing punitive, ever.** No streaks, no red "overdue," no rollover-shame. Undone tasks compost or roll forward without failure-affect (Afrofuturist-spiral: a missed task isn't a failure in linear-progress time, it's soil). *Violates:* any mechanic that scores June as behind/failing.
- **Offer, don't impose (crip-time temporal sovereignty).** Every adaptive feature offers a reading; June holds authority over her own time and state. Permission-granting register throughout ("you can… you don't have to…"), never imperative ("do this next" / "you're behind"). *Violates:* imperative/nagging push-language; capacity inference treated as automatic rather than correctable.
- **Capacity is multi-dimensional, never a single scalar.** *Violates:* collapsing energy/embodiment/etc. into one priority or readiness number (the output-format-bias flattening).
- **The anti-drift check must not become surveillance.** A system that breaks down tasks and monitors goal-alignment could reproduce the productivity-clock it's meant to refuse — the real political risk. Alignment-reflection is *offered* ("want to see how this week mapped to your goals?"), never a nagging scold. Naming the risk is the guard (PMA reflexivity).
- **Don't flatten June's situated specifics.** A task/project/day is never collapsed to a tidy number; generative/relational representation over scalar scoring (the master principle — see SYNTHESIS Part 4).

## 8.5 Added in QC + June's margin notes (2026-06-14)

**Further parameters surfaced in the warm QC** (the synthesis under-weighted these; a design agent should treat them as live):
- **Voice / messy-brain-dump input is core, not optional.** June's primary way of getting things out of her head is voice memos / unstructured dumps. The tool should accept a messy voice-or-text infodump and let the AI organize it into projects/tasks/steps — she does *not* pre-sort. (This is the STEP pattern, and it's exactly what June flags below in Goblin Tools' "magic to-do" / compiler and Taskmaster.)
- **Reminding without nagging — the central unsolved interface tension.** June forgets everything (so she needs reminding) but must not feel watched or nagged (the anxiety/productivity-clock she's escaping). *How* the system surfaces things to her — push? a calm dashboard she pulls from? a daily check-in? — is its own design parameter, and it sits right on the anti-surveillance guard (§6).
- **Interface + platform — including her sister's.** Where she touches this (terminal / web / phone) is undecided; calendar-sync is specifically a phone problem. And we need her sister's actual framework + what platform it runs on, since June is porting from it (credit the sister; it's a real source, not to reconstruct from memory).
- **Privacy / local-first.** Tasks include legal, job-search, and health material. Local-first (like Reframe and PMA), not a hosted to-do service. A hard constraint, likely a guard in §6.
- **Cold-start.** The learning loop has no data on day one; the system must be useful *before* it has learned her patterns (June's own Autograder solved a version of this with a Bayesian cold-start baseline).
- **Existing-tracker reconciliation (sharpens Param #14).** The global-vs-per-project question has concrete existing systems to reconcile: the job-search TRACKER, Canvas, Reframe's own Garden/OpenSpec task tooling, per-repo notes.

**June's margin-note questions (open design questions for the design agent, not yet resolved):**
- **Adopt / adapt / build — a real fork, surfaced by June.** Rather than building from scratch, evaluate existing neurodivergent tools — June named **Goblin Tools** (its "magic to-do" + compiler turn a brain-dump into steps — she wants exactly this) and **Taskmaster**; also worth checking **Tiimo** (autism/ADHD-designed). We might adopt or adapt one instead of building. This sidesteps the build-it-all-yourself dichotomy and fits "don't reinvent the wheel" — *and* raises a real question (an off-the-shelf tool won't carry the crip-time guards, the `reaching_for` layer, or the relational-project structure, so the likely shape is *adapt a brain-dump engine + build the goal-alignment/relational layer around it*). **First design-phase task: an adopt-vs-adapt-vs-build evaluation of these tools through the crip-time + output-format-bias lens.**
- **A project `parameters` field as an evolving framework — and granularity.** June asks whether a project should carry a `parameters` field (an evolving per-project framework, some of which emerges during design) — *or* whether that belongs in PMA, tied to specific project directories. And the sharp granularity question: **does "wash the dishes" need parameters at all?** This says: the full apparatus (`reaching_for`, parameters, capacity tagging) should be *optional / scaled to the task* — imposing heavy structure on a light task is its own kind of flattening from the other direction. Design implication: projects/tasks have a *variable level of structure*; a one-off chore stays a bare checkable item, a major project gets the full apparatus. (Leans toward: rich per-project framework = PMA's job at maturity, tied to project dirs; gsdo holds a thin/optional version — consistent with the §7 thin-now-rich-later seam.)

## 9. Decision provenance

Workshop Session 1, 2026-06-14 (this design built dialogically; June's corrections are load-bearing and credited): the reaching-for-as-ground-truth move (June); rest-is-checkable-with-inverted-friction (June, correcting Claude); the Python-core + AI-interface architecture (June); PMA option 3 (Claude proposed, June chose).

The workshop opened by surfacing four candidate answers to "what kind of object is this, fundamentally?" — (A) a system that watches what June is reaching for and reflects it back instead of her keeping a list; (B) a relational web of projects where "the next thing" emerges from where she is in each; (C) a capacity-first daily surface organized by what fits her body/energy/location right now; (D) a conventional task manager with the punitive parts removed. June's key move was to **dissolve the either/or**: because a to-do list has real stakes (tracking what's actually done matters, and she genuinely needs the list to externalize what she can/must do), the conventional checkable list (D) earns its place *here* and shouldn't be reinvented — while the relational-project structure (B) and capacity-first organization (C) layer on top, and the "watch what she's reaching for" idea (A) relocates to the AI layer doing the *how* (breaking tasks down, checking goal-alignment) rather than asking June to narrate intent she can't hold all at once. That synthesis is the design above.
