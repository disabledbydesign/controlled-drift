Controlled Drift · color system
A grammar for color, so your eye always knows where it is
The palette grew into ~10 competing bright hues with no logic between them — that's the noise. The fix is a grammar: spend saturation only where it carries meaning, keep everything else calm.
Structure = cool. The Map hierarchy is one blue family in lightness steps — depth is value, not a rainbow.
Execution = warm. Rose = why it matters · gold = do it. Warm vs cool = act-now vs the map.
Kind accents, sparingly. Only cross-cutting types (recurring, strategy) get a quiet hue.
State stays tiny. Done · now · blocked · parked — desaturated, never alarm-red.
1a Cool Deep
Calm & muted — lowest noise. My pick for the accessibility goal.
