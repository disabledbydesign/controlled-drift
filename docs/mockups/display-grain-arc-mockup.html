Task grain — the daily-life chore

“Clean the kitchen” shows as the one specific action, with an ordinary done-check. A chore is the whole thing — no arc to scan. Check it and it's done.

Block that opens to a task arc — leatherworking

Work that has real ordered steps opens to its arc: ✓ behind, the glowing rose step is where you are, ☐ ahead. No “2 done, 4 to go” — the rose is the position. Checking means “did a chunk today”; it stays open and returns tomorrow.

Block that's just a chunk — scholarly writing, dev

Nonlinear creative work (a stack of papers, dev threads) is just a chunk of time — no drill-down on the phone. You pick the thread; the deep structure lives in the project workspace (the agent-facing surface), not here. If a project has no clean task list yet, it simply stays a chunk — nothing breaks.

Which one a block is comes from the data — has its own tasks → opens; is a bundle of subprojects → stays a chunk. No hand-sorting. And the chunk length (2h / 4h) is yours; the system remembers what you keep setting.
